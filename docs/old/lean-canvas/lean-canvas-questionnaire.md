## 1. Problem

**Purpose:** Define the top 1-3 high-value problems your target customers are actively trying to solve right now.

### Questions

**1.1** What specific, repetitive, and high-cost software engineering or data analysis task is currently consuming significant developer-hours for my target customer?

**Answer:**

The top three high-cost, repetitive tasks consuming significant developer and analyst hours are:

**Problem 1 - Manual Data Sanitization:** Data analytics departments in mid-to-large enterprises spend up to 40% of their time on manual data janitorial work. This includes correcting inconsistencies, normalizing formats across disparate sources (e.g., CSVs, APIs, databases), and enriching records before any actual analysis can occur. This is a non-scalable bottleneck that directly inflates the cost of generating business intelligence.

**Problem 2 - Repetitive Internal Tooling:** Non-technical business units repeatedly request the creation of small, custom internal applications (e.g., automating report generation, streamlining data entry, creating simple dashboards). Engineering teams are pulled away from core product development to build and maintain these one-off tools, a process that is slow, costly, and often results in a backlog of unfulfilled requests from the business side.

**Problem 3 - Ad-Hoc AI Strategy & Governance:** Senior technical leadership and management spend countless hours in meetings trying to define AI usage policies, assess risks, and create governance frameworks from scratch. This unstructured, repetitive "meta-work" consumes strategic time that could be spent on innovation and execution, and it often results in policies that are disconnected from operational reality.
